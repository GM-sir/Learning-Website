  1. Semantic HTML


    Semantic HTML refers to using meaningful tags to structure web content. Below are examples and explanations: 
        <header>  
      <h1>Website Title</h1>  
      <nav>     
        <ul>      
          <li><a href="#home">Home</a></li>     
          <li><a href="#about">About</a></li>   
        </ul> 
      </nav>
    </header> 
    <main> 
      <section>   
        <h2>Introduction</h2>  
        <p>Welcome to the website.</p>  
      </section>  
      <article>   
        <h2>Latest Article</h2> 
        <p>Content of the article goes here.</p>  
      </article> 
    </main>
    <aside>
      <p>Related links or advertisements.</p>      
    </aside> 
    <footer> 
      <p>© 2024 Your Company</p>     
    </footer>
    • Importance: Semantic tags improve accessibility by providing clear structure for assistive technologies (like screen readers). They also enhance SEO by helping search engines better understand the content.

  2. Multimedia Integration

    HTML provides tags to embed multimedia content:
    • Adding Audio: 
       
            <audio controls> 
              <source src="audio-file.mp3" type="audio/mpeg">
            Your browser does not support the audio tag.
            </audio>
    • Adding Video: 
                   <video controls width="600">
               <source src="video-file.mp4" type="video/mp4">
              Your browser does not support the video tag.
             </video>
    • Embedding External Content: 
                   <iframe src="https://www.example.com" width="800" height="600"> 
              Your browser does not support iframes. 
             </iframe>

  3. Best Practices


    To ensure high-quality, maintainable HTML:
    • Proper Use of Indentation: 
                  
       
              <h1>Title</h1>
              <p>Content goes here.</p>             
            </div>
    • Comments:

      <!-- This is a comment. -->


    • Validating HTML Code: Use W3C Validator to check your HTML for errors and ensure best practices.
